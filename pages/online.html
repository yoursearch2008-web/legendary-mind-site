GTA Online vs GTA VI Online

10 years of content vs a fresh start. What actually matters when picking your online grind?

← Back to home

GTA Online in 2026

GTA Online launched in 2013 and Rockstar has been updating it ever since. We're talking hundreds of hours of content — heists, businesses, properties, vehicles, jobs. It's a full game on its own at this point. The player base is still massive, it runs on everything, and most of the content is accessible.

The grind is real though. Sharks cards (real money) are always hovering. New players in 2026 face a huge content wall.

GTA VI Online

Fresh slate. Rockstar learned from 10 years of GTA Online mistakes. Expect better matchmaking, less grind-heavy progression, and a more social experience. The Florida map opens up massive possibilities for new game modes. Early leaks suggest property systems, co-op heists, and dynamic world events.

The catch? It's year one. Content will be limited at launch vs what GTA Online has now.

The honest answer

Play GTA VI Online for the fresh start and the future. Keep GTA Online for when you want that decade of content. Both are worth your time fr fr.

Verdict: GTA Online for now, GTA VI Online for the future

At launch, GTA Online still wins on content volume. Give GTA VI Online 1–2 years of updates and it'll be the undisputed king. We are not okay with how good it's going to be ngl.
